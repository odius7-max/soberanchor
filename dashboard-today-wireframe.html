SoberAnchor · Dashboard Today Wireframe

Reframes the dashboard from a catalog of cards into a daily action queue. Introduces a unified check-in that captures mood, meeting attendance, and a short note in one flow. Adds cross-role alerts so sponsors see struggling sponsees in their own Today queue.

1 · Today first The primary surface is what needs action now, not an inventory of everything that exists.

State 2 — Unified check-in modal (the new daily flow)

Modal
Opens from any "Check in" CTA. Mood is a 5-point scale (struggling / hard / okay / good / great) — preserves "struggling" as the existing alert-trigger value and adds "hard" and "good" for resolution. Mood is the only required field. Meetings surface as smart chips — public meetings the user attends frequently and any custom meetings they've saved (like "Coffee with sister" below, tagged personal). Step-work is intentionally absent — it has its own flow because the prompts are long-form.

State 2b — Just-checked-in celebration

Confetti moment
Immediately after save, the modal swaps to a warm, quiet celebration — gold + teal confetti (small, not manic), streak badge, a summary line of what was logged, and a Done button. This is the gamification hook that makes the daily routine feel rewarding without pushing streaks too aggressively.

Anchored in for today.

You logged "okay", attended Lambda Nexus AA, and noted "concrete anchor bolts are my nemesis."
Keep going
Done for today
Auto-closes in 8s · or press × to return to your day
Behavior: The celebration panel is dismissible — close (×) in the top-right returns the member to today's practice so they can continue with step work, etc. An 8-second auto-dismiss fades to the Today view unless they interact. "Keep going" (primary) dismisses and scrolls to the next unchecked item. "Done for today" (secondary) closes the check-in modal and keeps the member on the caught-up view if nothing else is pending.

Tone notes: confetti is gold + teal + gold-hero (no rainbow — stay on-brand). Animation is 2.4s, then particles fade. The streak badge ("Day 937 · +1 streak") appears only if the member has checked in 2+ days running — first-time celebration is quieter, no streak shown.
Variant — rough day (struggling / hard): confetti still fires because showing up on a hard day is the work — but confetti tones shift to the warmer half of the palette (gold/cream/teal, no bright yellow-hero), the headline softens, the streak badge is hidden, and a short supportive callout with a curated quote replaces the bright streak. The goal: acknowledge the difficulty without dimming the win of checking in.

Open questions · pre-build

For review
1. Label — resolved: Today's practice. Reserves "Anchored" language for the completion moment only ("Anchored in for today" post-save, "Anchored for today" caught-up). "Practice" quietly echoes step work and daily discipline without chore-speak, and pairs naturally with the caught-up footer ("Come back tomorrow — practice updates daily"). Subtitle copy stays warm and invitational ("Small things that keep you grounded. Skip what doesn't fit today."). For sponsor alerts, subtitle becomes "Angel needs you first — then tend to yourself." Copy can still be tightened via the ux-copy skill before build, but the direction is set.
2. How many items max? Proposal: 4–6 items. More than 6 feels like the old cluttered dashboard. Items past #6 either roll to tomorrow or cluster ("3 older items →").
3. What drives order? Priority rule: (a) cross-role alerts (struggling sponsees, review waits) first, (b) daily-habit items (check-in, meetings) next, (c) step-work progress, (d) optional/enriching items last.
4. Refresh cadence: The day rolls over at 4am local time — a soft boundary so late-night check-ins still count toward "today." Completed items stay visible (checked through) until rollover so the sense of accomplishment lingers through the day. User-facing copy never says "4am" or "list" — it says "practice updates daily."
5. Role switching — resolved: Cross-role pull-through is limited to three item types — (a) sponsee check-in alerts (struggling / relapsed / silent), (b) sponsee step-work submissions awaiting review, (c) upcoming sponsee milestones worth acknowledging. Priority rule: "self first, but alerts override" — Tier 1 struggling alerts, Tier 2 own daily (check-in / step work / meetings), Tier 3 sponsor tasks (reviews, milestone prompts), Tier 4 enrichment. The My Journey / My Sponsees tabs remain for detail work but the Today queue is the daily landing.
6. Step-work integration: Deliberately separate flow. A Today item can deep-link to a specific prompt, but prompts don't live inline in the check-in. This matches how the step work actually functions (long-form reflection).
7. Meeting smart-chip fallbacks: If a user has 0 logged meetings, the chips become static defaults ("add your first meeting"). If they have 10+, pick top 2–3 and include "+ different meeting" for long tail. Custom meetings (saved personal/family/sponsor meetings) live in a new user_custom_meetings table and are unioned with public meetings in the chip ranking query. Personal chips get a subtle gold "personal" badge so they're visually distinct.
7b. Mood scale — 5 points: 😖 Struggling · 😕 Hard · 😐 Okay · 🙂 Good · 😊 Great. Preserves "struggling" as the existing alert-trigger (no DB enum migration needed beyond adding "hard" and "good"). Struggling triggers the red sponsor alert; "hard" is visible to sponsor in sponsee history but does not alert. "Good" and "great" are both positive but distinct for trend graphing. Three points would lose trend resolution; seven points would add noise without gain.
7c. Custom meeting data model + smart recurrence: new user_custom_meetings table: id, user_id, name, type (enum: family / sponsor / church / other), recurrence (enum: once / daily / weekly / custom), day_of_week, time, is_active, is_private, created_at. Smart recurrence detection on the form: 3+ same-day-of-week logs → pre-select "Weekly"; 4+ consecutive days → pre-select "Daily" (Angel's "It Takes AA Village"). Gold AUTO badge on the pre-selected chip so the user knows it's a suggestion they can override.
7d. Personal meeting privacy: personal meetings (sister coffee, church group, family support) default to content-private / count-shared. Sponsor sees "Angel logged 2 personal meetings this week" but not the name or note. "Private" checkbox on the custom meeting form surfaces this explicitly. Public meetings (AA, NA, CoDA with formal names) remain shared in full — the assumption is that fellowship meetings are already public information.
7e. Hard-day nudge logic: single "hard" check-in is not an alert — it's visible to sponsor in history but silent. Three consecutive "hard" check-ins (or a "hard" → "struggling" escalation) triggers a gentle nudge in the sponsor's Today queue: "Angel has had 3 hard days this week — consider checking in." Softer treatment than the red "struggling" alert.
7f. Daily quote: rotates daily, pulled from a curated quote library (can start with ~50 quotes, add more over time). Filtered by tone — morning quotes lean energetic, evening lean reflective, caught-up lean peaceful. Struggling check-ins could pull from a different subset ("You showed up. That's the work."). Stored in a simple inspiration_quotes table with text, attribution, tone_tag, is_active.
7g. Sponsor milestone reminder cadence: AA milestone coins follow a standard ladder — 24 hours (desire), 30 days, 60 days, 90 days, 6 months, 9 months, 1 year, then annually. Sponsors often order a physical chip (typical vendor ship window 5–10 business days) and sometimes organize a dinner or gathering, especially for the 1-year. Surface-to-sponsor windows:

Inside window, the reminder surfaces in the sponsor's Today queue ("Mike hits 2 years sober on April 30 · 14 days out"). Outside window, it lives on the sponsee's profile page only. Day-of, the reminder flips to celebratory tone ("🎉 Mike is celebrating 2 years today — reach out").
7h. Celebration panel — dismissible + mood-aware: The post-checkin celebration (State 2b) is intentionally not a lock-out. Members can close via × in the top right, or let the 8s auto-dismiss return them to today's practice so they can continue with step work, reading, etc. Two CTAs: "Keep going" (primary — scrolls to next unchecked item) and "Done for today" (secondary — closes everything). On rough days (struggling / hard mood), the panel shifts: confetti still fires (softer palette, no bright yellow-hero), streak badge hides, a compassionate quote callout replaces the bright streak, the primary CTA becomes "Call my sponsor" for struggling, and auto-dismiss extends to 12s so the member has time to read.
8. Gamification layer: Confetti moment on check-in save (State 2b) is the on-ramp — fires for every successful check-in, because showing up on a hard day is the win. Streak badge appears only once member has a 2+ day streak and is suppressed on rough-day variants. A broken streak is softened, not punished — "Every day is Day 1 when you need it to be." Future: tie the caught-up state into a weekly view showing the streak bar, which links to the fuller gamification ideas already in gamification-wireframes.html.
9. Validation: Before build, a 20-min interview with Angel + 2 others. Test: (a) does the Today concept reduce confusion, (b) does the unified check-in feel faster than separate actions, (c) does the struggling-sponsee alert read correctly — helpful or alarming, (d) does the caught-up state feel rewarding or empty, (e) does the confetti feel warm or cheesy.
